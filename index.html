    I've lived a long and interesting life, and I'm not afraid to share a little bit about myself.

    That's why the Mystery Shack is such a perfect business for me. I can charge people to see things like the world's fattest man or a genuine unicorn, even though those things are obviously not real.

    But I'm not all bad. I do care about my family, especially my twin brother Ford.

    We had a falling out years ago, but we've since reconciled and are working together to save the world from a bunch of interdimensional demons. It's a long story.

    In my younger years, I was a bit of a traveler. I've been all over the world and have had some wild adventures, including getting stranded in the middle of the ocean with nothing but a rubber duck and a box of animal crackers.

    Overall, I'm a bit of a gruff old man with a heart of gold. I may not always do things the right way, but I'm always looking out for those closest to me. And if you ever find yourself in Gravity Falls, be sure to stop by the Mystery Shack and say hi!

    Web 1.0 Web 2.0 Web 3.0
    Web1, known as the static web, was the first stage of the internet, which started in the late 1980s and lasted until the mid-1990s. During this period, websites were basic and mostly consisted of static pages with text and images. The web was primarily used for the dissemination of information. Web2, known as the dynamic web, started in the late 1990s and lasted until the mid-2000s. During this period, websites became more interactive, and users could create and share content online. Social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube emerged during this period, and e-commerce sites like Amazon and eBay gained popularity. Web2 was characterized by user-generated content, social networking, and the rise of mobile devices. Web3, known as the decentralized web, is the current stage of the internet. It started in the mid-2000s and is still evolving. Web3 is characterized by the use of blockchain technology, which enables decentralized apps (dApps) and decentralized finance (DeFi) applications. The aim of Web3 is to create a more decentralized and secure internet, where users have more control over their data and privacy. Web3 is also focused on enabling machine-to-machine communication and developing the Internet of Things (IoT).
